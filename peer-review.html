Peer Review Process & Policy

Ensuring scholarly integrity through transparent, ethical, and constructive review

Overview

Vivrtih Journal of Food Science and Clinical Nutrition follows a double-blind peer review model to uphold the highest standards of academic integrity. This process ensures that both authors and reviewers remain anonymous, promoting impartiality and fairness in manuscript evaluation.

Editorial Workflow

  • Submission Received: Manuscripts are submitted via the journal’s submission portal or designated email.
  • Managing Editor Assignment: The Managing Editor conducts an initial scope and formatting check, then assigns the manuscript to an appropriate Associate Editor or the Editor-in-Chief based on subject relevance.
  • Editorial Screening: The assigned editor evaluates the manuscript for thematic fit, ethical compliance, and readiness for peer review.
  • Reviewer Selection & Invitation: Qualified reviewers are invited based on expertise and absence of conflicts of interest.
  • Double-Blind Peer Review: Reviewers assess the manuscript anonymously. Authors do not know who reviewed their work.
  • Editorial Decision: Based on reviewer feedback, the editor issues a decision: Accept, Minor Revision, Major Revision, or Reject. The Editor may also refine or clarify reviewers’ comments, where necessary, to correct factual inaccuracies or omissions before they are communicated to authors.
  • Revision & Re-review: Minor revisions are typically reviewed editorially. Major revisions trigger a second round of peer review.
  • Final Acceptance & Proofing: Accepted manuscripts are copyedited and prepared for publication.

Reviewer Guidelines

Reviewers are expected to uphold the journal’s standards of scholarly rigor and ethical integrity. Key expectations include:

  • Evaluation Criteria:
    • Relevance: Alignment with journal scope and thematic focus
    • Originality & Contribution: Novel contributions or well-structured findings
    • Scientific Rigor: Sound methodology, data integrity, and analytical clarity
    • Clarity: Logical structure and quality of writing
    • Ethical Integrity: Adherence to research and publication ethics
    • Responsiveness: Reviewers’ comments must be constructive and sufficiently addressed by the authors during revision
  • Confidentiality: All manuscripts and review materials are confidential. Reviewers must not share or discuss content outside the review process.
  • Constructive Feedback: Reviews should be specific, respectful, and focused on improving the manuscript.
  • Conflict of Interest: Reviewers must disclose any potential conflicts and decline the review if impartiality is compromised.
  • Timeliness: Reviews are expected within 18 days of acceptance. Extensions may be granted upon request.

Estimated Total Review Cycle: 6–8 weeks from submission to final decision. Timelines may vary depending on manuscript complexity and reviewer availability.

Decision Thresholds

  • Acceptance: Requires at least two favourable reviews. Minor revisions may be accepted without re-review.
  • Revision: Major revisions trigger a second round of peer review. Once authors submit the revised manuscript, it will undergo a second round of review before a final decision.
  • Rejection: Manuscripts are rejected if both reviewers identify fundamental flaws in methodology, logic, or ethical compliance.

Ethical Standards

VJFSCN adheres to COPE (Committee on Publication Ethics) guidelines. All parties—authors, reviewers, and editors—are expected to uphold:

  • Integrity in research and reporting
  • Transparency in authorship and funding
  • Respect for intellectual property
  • Zero tolerance for plagiarism or data fabrication

Appeals & Revisions

Authors may appeal editorial decisions by submitting a detailed justification. Appeals are reviewed by the Editor-in-Chief and may take 3–4 weeks to process. The EiC’s decision is final.

During revision, authors must submit a point-by-point response to reviewer comments, clearly indicating how each concern has been addressed. Revised manuscripts should be submitted within the specified timeframe or authors may request an extension if needed.
