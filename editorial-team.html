Editorial Policy

Vivrtih Journal of Food Science and Clinical Nutrition is dedicated to ensuring the integrity, quality, and accessibility of the scientific content we publish. This policy serves as a comprehensive guide for authors, reviewers, editors, and readers regarding the journal’s editorial standards and practices.

We follow the ethical principles outlined by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and maintain transparency at every stage of the editorial and publication process.

Scope and Objectives

The journal focuses on advancing research in the fields of clinical nutrition, food science, and health science disciplines. Our objectives are to:

  • Promote the dissemination of innovative research.
  • Provide a platform for global collaboration and knowledge exchange.
  • Enhance awareness and understanding of critical topics in nutrition and food science.

Editorial Independence

The editorial team operates independently to ensure unbiased decision-making. Editorial decisions are based on the scientific rigor, originality, and relevance of submissions, free from influence by publisher.

Appeals and Complaints

The authors have the right to appeal if they have a genuine cause to believe that the editorial board has wrongly rejected the paper. If the authors wish to appeal the decision, they should email the editorial office [Email of journal] explaining in detail the reason for the appeal. The appeals will be acknowledged by the editorial office and will be investigated in an unbiased manner. The processing of appeals will be done within 6–8 weeks. While under appeal, the said manuscript should not be submitted to other journals. The final decision rests with the Editor-in-Chief. Second appeals are not considered.

Disclaimer

The views and findings expressed in published articles are those of the authors and do not necessarily reflect the journal’s or publisher’s opinions.

Amendments to the Policy

VJFSCN reserves the right to amend or update Policies of the journal at any time to comply with evolving laws or best practices. All changes will be published on the journal, and authors and contributors are encouraged to review the policy regularly.

Editorial Board Responsibilities

  • Overseeing the peer review process to ensure fairness and rigor.
  • Assisting with ethical and professional queries or disputes.
  • Contributing to the journal’s strategic development and promotion.
  • Supporting authors with feedback and guidance.

Editor-in-Chief

  • Oversees editorial strategy and ensures high editorial standards and overall quality.
  • Manages the peer review process when required.
  • Handles complex cases, ethical issues, and disputes.
  • Editor-in-Chief’s decision is final.

Associate Editors

  • Manage the peer review process.
  • Select appropriate reviewers and evaluate their feedback.
  • Associate Editor has discretion to accept, reject, or modify reviewers’ comments before communicating to the managing editor.
  • Make the final decision on manuscripts.
  • Editors associated with any manuscript shall not be involved in its editorial process.

Managing Editors

  • Handle administrative tasks, including communication with authors.
  • Assign manuscripts to editors.
  • Ensure timely processing and adherence to guidelines.

Peer Review Policy

A manuscript will be reviewed for publication with the understanding that it is being submitted solely to Vivrtih Journal of Food Science and Clinical Nutrition and has not been published, simultaneously submitted, or accepted elsewhere. Authors should authorize one correspondent for all manuscript-related communication. All manuscripts received are duly acknowledged.

We employ a double-blind peer review process to ensure fairness and objectivity. Manuscripts with insufficient originality, serious flaws, or a lack of a significant message may be rejected during initial editorial screening. Editorial board members who are authors will be excluded from publication decisions.

Peer Review Process

  1. Step 1: Manuscript Submission – Authors submit manuscripts via the journal’s online portal or email.
  2. Step 2: Initial Screening – Managing Editor checks for plagiarism, ethical violations, scope adherence, and submission guideline compliance.
  3. Step 3: Assignment to Associate Editors – Manuscripts assigned to relevant editors based on expertise.
  4. Step 4: Peer Review Process – Manuscripts are reviewed by two or more qualified reviewers. Reviewers evaluate scientific validity, originality, methodology, and clarity.
  5. Step 5: Editorial Decision – Associate Editor reviews feedback and makes a final decision.
  6. Step 6: Copyediting & Publication – Accepted manuscripts undergo copyediting and formatting before online publication.

Review and Decision Criteria

  • Relevance – Alignment with journal scope and focus.
  • Originality/Contribution – Novel findings or well-structured replication.
  • Scientific Rigor – Accurate and well-structured methods, data, and analysis.
  • Clarity – Well-written, logically organized manuscript.
  • Ethical Integrity – Adherence to research and publishing ethics.
  • Constructive Feedback – Reviewer comments must be constructive and addressed by authors.

Decision Thresholds

  • Acceptance – At least two positive reviews; minor revisions allowed.
  • Revision – If one reviewer requests major revisions, authors submit revised manuscript for second review. Associate Editor can modify reviewer comments before sending to authors.
  • Rejection – Both reviewers recommend rejection due to fundamental flaws, or if ethical standards are not met. Associate Editor makes final decision; Editor-in-Chief’s decision is final if assigned directly.

Correction and Retraction Policy

Corrections: Issued when published information accuracy is compromised. Published in subsequent issues under “Corrections” and “Addenda.”

Retractions: Issued when fundamental conclusions are seriously undermined. Maintains scientific record integrity.
