Editorial Policy

Vivrtih Journal of Food Science and Clinical Nutrition is dedicated to ensuring the integrity, quality, and accessibility of the scientific content we publish. This policy serves as a comprehensive guide for authors, reviewers, editors, and readers regarding the journal’s editorial standards and practices. We follow the ethical principles outlined by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE) and maintain transparency at every stage of the editorial and publication process.

The journal focuses on advancing research in the fields of clinical nutrition, food science, and health science disciplines.

  • Promote the dissemination of innovative research.
  • Provide a platform for global collaboration and knowledge exchange.
  • Enhance awareness and understanding of critical topics in nutrition and food science.

The editorial team operates independently to ensure unbiased decision-making. Editorial decisions are based on the scientific rigor, originality, and relevance of submissions, free from influence by publisher.

The authors have the right to appeal if they have a genuine cause to believe that the editorial board has wrongly rejected the paper. If the authors wish to appeal the decision, they should email the editorial office [Email of journal] explaining in detail the reason for the appeal. The appeals will be acknowledged by the editorial office and will be investigated in an unbiased manner. The processing of appeals will be done within 6 – 8 weeks. While under appeal, the said manuscript should not be submitted to other journals. The final decision rests with the Editor in Chief. Second appeals are not considered.

The views and findings expressed in published articles are those of the authors and do not necessarily reflect the journal’s or publisher’s opinions.

VJFSCN reserves the right to amend or update Policies of the journal at any time to comply with evolving laws or best practices. All changes will be published on the journal, and authors and contributors are encouraged to review the policy regularly.

A manuscript will be reviewed for publication with the understanding that it is being submitted to Vivrtih Journal of Food Science and Clinical Nutrition alone at that point in time and has not been published anywhere, simultaneously submitted, or already accepted for publication elsewhere. Authors should authorize one of them to correspond with the Journal for all matters related to the manuscript. All manuscripts received are duly acknowledged.

Our journal employs a double-blind peer review process to ensure fairness and objectivity. All submitted manuscripts are initially reviewed by editors for suitability for formal review. Manuscripts with insufficient originality, serious flaws, or a lack of a significant message are rejected at this stage. Manuscripts that are in the scope of the journal are also liable to be rejected at this stage itself. Manuscripts received from Editorial Board members will be screened by the Editor in Chief or other Associate Editors and sent to external peer reviewers. The editorial board members who are authors will be excluded from publication decisions.

  1. Step 1: Manuscript Submission. Authors submit their manuscripts via the journal’s online portal or email.
  2. Step 2: Initial Screening. The Managing Editor checks for plagiarism and ethical violations, and verifies that the manuscript is within the journal's scope and follows submission guidelines.
  3. Step 3: Assignment to Associate Editors. The Managing Editor assigns manuscripts to the relevant Associate Editor or the Chief Editor based on subject expertise.
  4. Step 4: Peer Review Process. The Associate Editor assigns the manuscript to two qualified reviewers or more when required. Reviewers evaluate the manuscript for scientific validity, originality, methodology, and clarity of writing. They then submit a recommendation to accept, reject, or request revisions.
  5. Step 5: Editorial Decision. The Associate Editor reviews the feedback and makes a final decision.
  6. Step 6: Copyediting & Publication. Once accepted, the manuscript undergoes copyediting for formatting and language, and is then published online.
  • Relevance – The study must align with the journal's scope and focus.
  • Originality/Contribution – The research should contribute novel findings, validate previous studies through replication, or present well-structured findings
  • Scientific Rigor – The methods, data, and analyses must be accurate and well-structured.
  • Clarity – The manuscript should be well-written and logically organized.
  • Ethical Integrity – The manuscript must adhere to ethical research and publishing standards.
  • Constructive Feedback: – Peer reviewers’ comments must be constructive and sufficiently addressed by the authors.
  • Acceptance: A manuscript requires at least two positive reviews for acceptance. Minor revisions may be accepted if reviewers find no significant flaws.
  • Revision: If at least one reviewer requests major revisions, the author must submit a revised manuscript addressing all concerns. This revised manuscript will undergo a second round of review before a final decision. The Associate Editor has full authority to modify reviewer comments before communicating them to the authors.
  • Rejection: A manuscript will be rejected if both reviewers recommend rejection due to fundamental flaws. Additionally, if a manuscript does not meet ethical standards, the Editor may reject it directly.
  • The Associate Editor makes the final decision on a manuscript based on reviewer feedback and journal policies. In cases of conflicting reviews, the Associate Editor may consult the Editor-in-Chief for guidance. When a manuscript is assigned directly or recommended to the Editor-in-Chief, their decision is final.

Corrections:

Corrections are issued when the accuracy of published information is significantly compromised. These corrections are published in a subsequent issue of the journal under the sections “Corrections” and “Addenda.”

Retractions:

Retractions occur when the fundamental conclusions of a paper are seriously undermined. This policy ensures that the integrity of the scientific record is maintained by addressing significant issues that impact the reliability of the research.
